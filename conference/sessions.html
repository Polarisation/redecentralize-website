Examples of Pre-Submitted Sessions

  • Mapping the space: Ira & Ross

    What's the status of various projecs? Trying things out and creating a wiki/db of alternatives: https://redecentralize.github.io/alternative-internet/.

  • What does decentralised news look like?: Bill Thompson

    "If you tell the kids these days that we had NNTP back in the 1980's they won't believe you..." Exploring ways to resist platform thinking and centralised news.

  • Rethinking interfaces to encourage decentralization: Philip Sheldrake

    Centralization talks in terms of users and user interfaces. Decentralization recognises the human with the advent of the human interface (HI). This session presents the hi:project (www.hi-project.org) and its contribution to redecentralization.

  • Decentralised personalisation, or: Why your decentralised service is not sticky: Benjamin HeitmannLet's assume that you can build a decentralised service, which is well engineered and solves a real problem. There is a big chance that it will still be less attractive compared to its mainstream and centralised competitors. Why? Because it will lack the stickiness of centralised services which is caused by social features and personalisation. On current social media sites, personalisation is a commodity, which is to say that it is an expected feature. Yet, almost all state-of-the-art personalisation approaches require centralised data collection. Join me for this talk, in order to learn about why decentralising personalisation is a big and unsolved challenge, and help with brainstorming possible solutions.
  • The Distributed Secure Social Web: Henry Story

    Redecentralising the Social Web is key to allowing individuals and organisations to co-operate dynamically and efficiently. By allowing people to own their data and link across organisational boundaries one can create a new economy of just-in-time projects, without needing to centralise everything on to the one big brother platform. This means building a web where every person, family or organisation can control their data on their server, specify who can see what, and easily linking to data on other servers. This linked data can then be used to generate dynamic user interfaces that are user friendly. A number of standards have been developed to do this at the W3C over the past 20 years, and are coming together under the SoLiD project.

  • Data ownership in the age of silos: Andrei Sambra, Nicola Greco

    This talk aims to introduce a new architecture that will radically change the way Web applications work today, resulting in true data ownership as well as improved privacy. It gives users the freedom to choose where their data resides and who is allowed to access it by decoupling content from the application itself. Because applications are decoupled from the data they produce, users will be able to avoid vendor lock-in by seamlessly switching the apps and personal data storage servers, without losing any data or social connections. Finally, it allows developers to easily innovate by creating new apps or improving current apps, all while reusing existing data that was created by other apps.

  • OwnCloud on your Raspberry Pi: Ed CromptonDitch Dropbox and learn the basics you need to set up a file server hosted in your own home at minimal cost. This talk is the step by step guide you'll need to turn your out-of-the-box Raspberry Pi into an OwnCloud server to share files from anywhere. Using the Raspian operating system, you'll learn how to install the necessary Free and Open Source software packages to get your file server up and running. You'll also come away with the knowledge required to securely access your server using your own domain name over your home internet connection. In addition we'll look at the basic features of OwnCloud and how you may want to extend it.
  • Decentralization: Ten Years of Standardization Failure and Our Last Best Hope: Harry HalpinOpenID, the Semantic Web, and now the blockchain. For decades, we've been trying to redecentralize the web, and our efforts have failed to reach widespread adoption. It's time for a new strategy.
