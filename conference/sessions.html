Examples of Pre-Submitted Sessions

  • Mapping the space: Ira & Ross What's the status of various projecs? Trying things out and creating a wiki/db of alternatives: https://redecentralize.github.io/alternative-internet/.
  • What does decentralised news look like?: Bill Thompson "If you tell the kids these days that we had NNTP back in the 1980's they won't believe you..." Exploring ways to resist platform thinking and centralised news.
  • Rethinking interfaces to encourage decentralization: Philip Sheldrake Centralization talks in terms of users and user interfaces. Decentralization recognises the human with the advent of the human interface (HI). This session presents the hi:project (www.hi-project.org) and its contribution to redecentralization.
  • The Distributed Secure Social Web: Henry Story Redecentralising the Social Web is key to allowing individuals and organisations to co-operate dynamically and efficiently. By allowing people to own their data and link across organisational boundaries one can create a new economy of just-in-time projects, without needing to centralise everything on to the one big brother platform. This means building a web where every person, family or organisation can control their data on their server, specify who can see what, and easily linking to data on other servers. This linked data can then be used to generate dynamic user interfaces that are user friendly. A number of standards have been developed to do this at the W3C over the past 20 years, and are coming together under the SoLiD project.
  • Data ownership in the age of silos: Andrei Sambra, Nicola Greco This talk aims to introduce a new architecture that will radically change the way Web applications work today, resulting in true data ownership as well as improved privacy. It gives users the freedom to choose where their data resides and who is allowed to access it by decoupling content from the application itself. Because applications are decoupled from the data they produce, users will be able to avoid vendor lock-in by seamlessly switching the apps and personal data storage servers, without losing any data or social connections. Finally, it allows developers to easily innovate by creating new apps or improving current apps, all while reusing existing data that was created by other apps.
